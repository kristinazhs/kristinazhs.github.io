Let's talk

I didn't come to B Corp™ certification through sustainability theory. I came through marketing and sales — eight years as a trade marketing manager, first in Russia, then in Brazil as part of Bonduelle's international team.

When Bonduelle Brasil decided to pursue B Corp™ certification, I took on the project. For ten months, I coordinated teams across sales, production, purchasing, HR, finance and general management — working in both English and Portuguese — and led the company through every stage of the process, from the first assessment to the final audit. We achieved certification with a score of 85 points.

So many good practices already existed inside the company — processes protecting natural resources, programmes supporting women in the workplace, a genuine commitment to diversity. B Corp™ certification didn't create these things. It gave them structure, language, and recognition.

That experience convinced me that the best companies pursue certification not to tick a box — the motivation comes from within. Certification gives it a framework, a discipline, and a way to prove it to the world. That's what I now help other companies build.

Services

Your B Corp™ Journey, Step by Step

Every company's path to certification is different. Each stage is available as a standalone service — start where you are, and decide as you go.

1

Stage one

Discovery

Not sure where to start? Start here. I walk you through what B Corp™ certification actually involves — the benefits, the realistic challenges, the timeline, and what happens even after you receive your certificate. By the end, you'll know exactly what you're signing up for.

2

Stage two

Diagnostics — Readiness Assessment & Gap Analysis

We identify which certification track fits your company and run a full gap analysis against the current B Impact Assessment (BIA) requirements. This stage answers the most important question honestly: is certification realistically achievable for you right now?

3

Stage three

Roadmap

Together we build a prioritized improvement roadmap — what needs to happen first, which departments are involved, and what processes may need to be introduced before the formal certification work begins.

4

Stage four

Implementation

This is the hands-on stage. I work directly with your teams to put the roadmap into action — coordinating across departments, tracking progress, and keeping leadership informed at every step.

5

Stage five

Submission & Audit

I lead the full submission process: completing the B Impact Assessment platform, gathering and organizing supporting evidence, and guiding your company through the audit itself.

6

Stage six

Post-Audit & Ongoing Compliance

Certification isn't the finish line. I help you address any non-conformities flagged by auditors and support your recertification — including surveillance audits at year three and year five, and in-person visits when required.

B Corp™ certification is awarded by B Lab to companies that meet rigorous standards of social and environmental performance, accountability and transparency. Certified B Corps commit to balancing purpose and profit — for their workers, communities, customers and the planet.

A consultant guides your company through every stage of the certification process — from understanding the B Impact Assessment (BIA) requirements, to running a gap analysis, building an improvement roadmap, coordinating implementation across departments, and managing the submission and audit.

Most companies take between six months and a year, depending on size, how many departments are involved, and how much groundwork already exists. Some companies take longer if significant process changes are needed first. At Bonduelle Brasil, we achieved certification in 10 months.

You can self-manage certification, and some smaller companies do. A consultant typically helps you move faster, avoid common pitfalls, and reduce the back-and-forth that often comes with a first submission — especially valuable if your internal team is already stretched thin.

My fees are monthly and scale with your company's size and certification track. Smaller companies on simpler tracks pay less; larger companies with more departments and complexity pay more. And because each stage is available separately, you're never committed to more than you need. Let's discuss your situation in a free call.

B Lab updated its certification framework significantly in 2025. Instead of reaching a score of 80 points, companies must now meet mandatory performance requirements across seven impact areas. This makes the process more rigorous — and expert guidance more valuable than ever. I completed B Lab Europe's training on the new standards in 2026.
